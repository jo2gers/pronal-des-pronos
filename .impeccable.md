## Design Context

### Users
Public audience of French-speaking football fans who want to compete at predicting World Cup 2026 match scores. Casual fans through to die-hards; they use the app in the evening before and during matches, on mobile and desktop. The competitive leaderboard and friend groups make social pressure a core engagement driver.

### Brand Personality
Competitive · Premium · Clean

Think: a real betting platform's discipline and precision, minus the gambling sleaze. The interface should feel like it was built by a startup that takes design seriously — not a weekend project with Tailwind defaults.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark — users are watching matches in the evening
- **Colors**: Move away from green/yellow Tailwind defaults entirely. Target: a deep neutral base (near-black, navy, or very dark slate) with a single sharp accent — consider electric orange, cool red, or crisp white-gold. No cyan-purple AI palette.
- **Feel**: Clean, structured, confident. Sparse whitespace with deliberate hierarchy. Dense information without feeling cluttered.
- **Anti-references**: Generic Tailwind green/yellow sports apps. AI dashboard aesthetics (cyan glows, purple gradients). Busy gambling sites with too many colors.

### Design Principles
1. **One accent, used sparingly** — a single accent color does all the heavy lifting. Overuse kills it.
2. **Information density with breathing room** — this is a data-heavy app (scores, odds, rankings). Density is a feature, not a problem. But every element must have intentional spacing.
3. **Competitive tension** — the UI should feel like there are stakes. Sharp edges, strong type, confident hierarchy.
4. **Mobile-first, match-night ready** — most users will check on their phone during a match. Legibility at a glance is non-negotiable.
5. **No decoration for its own sake** — every visual element must earn its place. No rounded-card-with-icon patterns, no gradient text, no decorative side stripes.
